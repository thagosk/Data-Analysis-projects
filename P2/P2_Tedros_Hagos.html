In, this project, I will use the titanic data which Contains demographics and passenger information from 891 of the 2224 . As described from the Kaggle website, the titanic sank after it colided with an iceberg on April, 15, 1912 which resulted in the overall death of 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew. The question on the project for the titanic dataset is "What factors made people more likely to survive?". Based on this broader question, I will try to refine my questions to answer some of these questions:

  1. Who were the passengers on the titanic (age, sex, name...)
  2. How does class affect, survival of passengers?
  3. Is there any survival rate differece between male and female passengers?
  4. is survival affected by age ( is there any previlaged age group?)
  5. who survived most, single passengers or passengers with family mebers on board?
  6. Does paying higher fare meant better survival rate?

Let us conduct test-statistic to dig more and find out if there is really survival diffefrence between gender.

Our hypothesis testing will be Ho:Pf=Pm: percentage of the population surivors for both gender is the same H1:Pf > Pm: percentage of the population surivors for both female is greater than male

In [11]:
#to counduct our test-statistic, we use the chi-square contingency
chi2_contingency(pd.crosstab(titanic_df.Survived, titanic_df.Sex))

The chi-square p-value (1.1973570627755645e-58), is alomt 0, which at 95% confidence interval, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the percentage of population survived for female is greater male.

conclusion¶

Summary of Findings¶

Our findings can be summerized in the following short ways:

* female passengers  had higher survival rate (74%) than male passengers (19%)
* Passengers in class 1 (63%) had higher survival chance than in class 2 (7%) or class 3 (24%)
* Ticket-fare of passengers greatly vary (ranges from 0-512) among passengers and it tends to have those who
    paid more had better survival rate.

Limitations¶

In conducting this research, I used the titanic data set with some missing values. The missing values especially for the age and cabin makes our research somehow unreliable. It was difficult to fill the missing value in the case of cabins, so I was forced to drop them only. In the case of missing values for age, I filled it with mean age, which may be appropriate for educational purposes only. Furthermore, as stated in the Kaggle website ages with fractional values were estimations (which may not be exact age of passengers).

Our data (and our few variables) do not help us to investigate more and get an answer why survival was affected by one variable: for example our data indicates hihger survival rate for class 1 than class 2 or class 3, but it does not tell if it was merely due to luck (less number of passengers, its height from the water body, which may help passengers to stay on top of water till they got aid) or if class 1 was equiped with better equipments to survive the danger (number of lifeboats in that class etc). So we dont have concrete answer to why survival rate was different among different variables.
